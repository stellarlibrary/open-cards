# Card 6: One-sided limits

**Section:** Limits and Continuity

## Front

What are left-hand and right-hand limits?

## Back

Left-hand limit:

$$\lim_{x\to a^-} f(x)$$

means $x$ approaches $a$ from values less than $a$.

Right-hand limit:

$$\lim_{x\to a^+} f(x)$$

means $x$ approaches $a$ from values greater than $a$.

The two-sided limit exists only if:

$$\lim_{x\to a^-} f(x)=\lim_{x\to a^+} f(x)$$
