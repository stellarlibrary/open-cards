# Card 8: Indeterminate form 0/0

**Section:** Limits and Continuity

## Front

What should you do when direct substitution gives $0/0$?

## Back

The form $0/0$ is indeterminate. Try:

- Factoring
- Rationalizing
- Simplifying algebraically
- Trig identities
- L'Hôpital's Rule, if allowed

Example:

$$\lim_{x\to 2}\frac{x^2-4}{x-2}$$

Factor:

$$\frac{(x-2)(x+2)}{x-2}=x+2$$

So the limit is:

$$4$$
