# Card 25: Logarithmic differentiation

**Section:** Derivatives

## Front

When is logarithmic differentiation useful?

## Back

Use logarithmic differentiation when a function has:

- Products
- Quotients
- Powers with variables in the base and exponent

Example:

$$y=x^x$$

Take natural log:

$$\ln y=x\ln x$$

Differentiate:

$$\frac{y'}{y}=\ln x+1$$

So:

$$y'=x^x(\ln x+1)$$
